Predictive Neuroimaging Lab

The Predictive Neuroimaging Lab of the University Hospital Essen is an interdisciplinary junior research group lead by Tamas Spisak.

The lab focuses on predictive modelling in neuroimaging (functional and structural MRI) and conducts research and development aiding the smooth fusion of the latest advances of machine learning and artificial intelligence into brain imaging research. We focus on the followings.

Brain Network Analysis

Exploit the rich information in structural and functional (dynamic) brain network data to build predictive models.

Methodologycal innovations

Develop novel feature engineering methods helping the fusion of machine learning techniques and neuroimaging

Neuromarkers

Develop novel canditates for imaging-based and multi-modal composite biomarkers in the context of cognition, learning, pain and placebo.

Research Products

Share and deploy predictive models and methodological developments as software tools (e.g. BIDS-apps)

Research & Development

RPN-signature

A non-invasive brain-based predictive signature of individual pain sensitivity.

pTFCE

Probabilistic Threshold-Free Cluster Enhancement of Neuroimages.

PUMI

A nipype-based modular pipeline development system focusing on connectivity-based predictive modelling.

FlexAct

Flexible modelling of spatial inaccuracies in individual activation maps with machine learning. In progress...

Motion Confounds in Predictive Modelling

Precise assesment of motion confounds in predictive modelling. In progress...

Palcebo Meta-Prediction

Meta-analysis and prediction of placebo response in collaboration with the Bingel-Lab and the placebo imaging collaboration. In progress...
